Step 01 — Classify the Exposure
● Category I
Touching / Feeding
Touching or feeding animals, licks on intact skin.
e.g. Feeding dog by hand, lick on unbroken skin
◆ Category II
Minor Scratch / Nibble
Nibbling of uncovered skin, minor scratches or abrasions without bleeding.
e.g. Superficial scratch, nibble with no blood
■ Category III
Bite / Deep Scratch
Single or multiple transdermal bites or scratches, licks on broken skin, saliva on mucous membranes.
e.g. Puncture wound, bleeding bite, saliva in eye/mouth
Option A — Standard
Intramuscular (IM)
Essen regimen · Deltoid · 4 or 5 doses
Option B — NCDC approved · Cost-saving
Intradermal (ID)
Updated Thai Red Cross · 2-2-2-0-2 · 0.1 mL × 2 sites

    Important Clinical Notes
    ⚠ Never Delay

    Begin PEP immediately. Rabies is 100% fatal once symptoms appear.

    🐾 10-Day Rule

    If animal stays healthy 10 days post-bite, vaccine may be stopped (if RIG not given).

    🩹 No Primary Suture

    Do NOT suture primarily. Delay closure 3–5 days with antibiotic cover.

    💉 Tetanus

    Always assess tetanus status. Give TT/TIG as appropriate.

    🦠 Antibiotics

    Prescribe antibiotics to prevent secondary bacterial infection.

    🏥 Free at Govt.

    ARV and RIG are available free at all government hospitals in India.
